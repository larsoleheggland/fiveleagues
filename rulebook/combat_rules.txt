# Combat Rules

## The Battle Round
Battles are a series of battle rounds, each with 5 phases:
1. The Initiative Roll
2. Quick Actions Phase
3. Enemy Actions Phase
4. Slow Actions Phase
5. Tracking Phase

## The Initiative Roll
Roll D6 per warband character/ally on table, assign each to a character:
- Die result ≤ Agility = Quick Actions Phase
- All enemies = Enemy Actions Phase
- Die result > Agility = Slow Actions Phase
Every character acts every round - roll just determines phase.

## Actions
During a phase, player characters activate in any order. Enemy phase: ranged before melee.
When activated: Move Action, then Combat Action OR Non-Combat Action.
- Combat Actions: melee combat or ranged attack
- Non-Combat Actions: Dash, Use, Ready Weapon, Casting, Interact, Keep Down!, Anticipate
Cannot change sequence to take Move last. Can skip any action.

## Non-Combat Actions Table
- Dash (full move): Move additional distance = Dash portion of Speed. Cannot enter melee by Dashing. Enemies Dash if in open reaching Cover or advancing on foe. Undead/Monster/Aberration cannot Dash.
- Use (full move): Use one item per Action.
- Ready Weapon (full move): Retrieve/return Backpack weapon.
- Casting (full move): Mystic casts spell.
- Interact (half move): Scenario objectives, cut rope, pick lock, search, etc.
- Keep Down! (1"): Cover from ranged attacks >6" away until next activation.
- Anticipate (1"): Place marker. Next round, roll one less die for initiative. This character auto-acts in Quick Actions Phase.

## Tracking Phase
End-of-round activities including enemy Morale checks. Resolve in order:
1. Warband-related features
2. Enemy-related features
3. Scenario/event features
4. Anything else
Within each step, player chooses order.

## Movement
- Movement allowance = Speed in inches
- Difficult terrain: 1" moved costs 2" of allowance
- Opening door/window: 1" of movement
- Cannot move through another figure unless rule permits

### Moving and Shooting
Ranged attack = max half movement Speed. Crossbow = must be stationary.

### Dash
Non-Combat Action. Additional distance = Dash bonus. Cannot enter melee by Dashing.

### Movement Modifiers
Expressed as +X"/+Y" (base/Dash). Example: Speed 4"/+3" = 4" normal, 7" when Dashing.

### Moving into Base Contact
Contact = bases touching (or within 0.5" for dramatic poses). Triggers melee immediately. Dashing figures cannot enter melee.

### Moving Vertically
Pay vertical height in inches. Can't end mid-climb. Drop ≤1" = free. Drop >1" = must climb down or jump.

#### Distance Fallen Table
- Up to 3": +1/+0 damage
- Up to 5": +2/+1 damage
- Up to 8": +3/+2 damage
- Over 8": Automatic casualty
Jumping/falling >1" = no more Actions this round. Can leap 2" gap as normal movement.

### Dungeon Floor Tiles
Individual debris costs 1" to cross. Rubble/water = Difficult. Height difference = 1" up, free down.

### Taking Damage while Moving
Possible from falls, traps, etc. Resolve per damage rules.

## Enemy Movements

### Order of Activation
Ranged before melee. Within each type: closest to player's battlefield edge first, work backwards.

### Ranged Troops
Move to Cover where they can shoot. Only advance to maintain range/sight. Won't leave Cover if visible to opposing ranged. Won't enter positions preventing shooting (water, etc.). Out of ammo = become melee.

### Melee Troops
Advance toward nearest opponents staying in Cover from ranged. If can't advance without leaving Cover, take fastest/most direct path. If can't reach nearest, target closest reachable opponent.

### Leaders
Given choice, attack Hero over Follower.

### Monsters and Undead
Ignore tactics, advance directly toward closest opponent. Won't enter dangerous terrain.

### Sensible Foes
Player judgment: enemies aren't suicidal. Don't make obviously suicidal moves.

## Unknown Enemy Markers
Token roughly figure-base sized. Move 5"/round toward center, then random direction.
Spotted when warband figure within: 6" (partially obscured) or 9" (unobscured LoS).
When spotted, roll D6: 1=1 foe, 2=2, 3-4=3, 5=4, 6=5. Always melee. Place in/behind Cover.

## Terrain
### Types
- Linear: long features (hedgerows, fences, walls)
- Area: cover space, figures placed within (forest, bushes, rubble). May be Difficult.
- Field: ground-level area (mud, water, lava). May be Difficult or Impassable.
- Individual: single piece, can't climb (barrels, trees, statues)
- Block: can be climbed but not entered (boulders, locked buildings)
- Interior: enclosed, can be entered (buildings, tunnels, caves)

### Lines of Sight
True Line of Sight. Blocked by Block/Individual features.
- Area terrain: LoS passes through up to 3" only
- Linear: LoS blocked 3" beyond, unless shooter or target within 1" of crossing point
- When in doubt about visibility: allow shot. When in doubt about Cover: count as Cover.

### Cover
A figure is in Cover if:
- LoS crosses terrain >1" from shooter
- Target within Area feature
- Target in contact with corner of Block terrain

### Location and Item Markers
Don't block LoS, provide Cover, or interfere with ranged. Enemies don't interact with them. Can't be spell targets.

## Ranged Attacks
Select visible target in range. If visible opponents within 3", must shoot nearest.

### Enemy targeting priorities:
1. Closest within 8"
2. Nearest with ranged weapon
3. Closest target

### To Hit (D6):
- Within 6", in open: 3+
- Within weapon range, in open: 5+
- Within range, concealed/Cover: 6+
- Stationary shooter adds Combat Skill
- Natural 1 always misses
- Limited Ammunition: natural 1 to Hit = out of ammo (unless first shot in battle)

## Melee Combat
Series of 3 exchanges. Initiator = attacker in first exchange.

### Each exchange has 5 steps:
1. Select weapon (1st exchange only) - choose one melee weapon for all exchanges
2. Select tactic (optional) - one tactic for all exchanges. Enemy doesn't use tactics.
   - Fight defensively: roll twice pick best, inflict no Hits, negates Counter Attack
   - Fight evasively: roll normally, inflict no Hits, may move 2" ending melee, negates Counter Attack
   - Fight furiously (attacker only): roll normally; win = +1 to Overcome Armor OR Toughness (player's choice); lose = suffer Hit
3. Resolve exchange: D6 + Combat Skill each.
   - Attacker higher: strikes blow, remains Attacker
   - Defender higher: avoids harm, becomes Attacker
   - Draw: Defender retreats 1", Attacker stays, melee ends
4. The Push: If Defender lost, retreats 1". Attacker may stay (end melee) or step up 1" (continue). Enemies always step up. Back to the Wall: if Defender can't retreat, stay in place, become Stunned, fight extra exchange.
5. Next Exchange: Winner has Initiative. After 3rd exchange, melee ends, each moves 1" apart.

### Counter Attack
Character with Counter Attack may strike Hit when winning exchange even as Defender.
Conditions: higher ground, fighting across obstacle, opponent in Difficult/water, or weapon/trait grants it.

### Parry
If character with Parry rolls natural 1 defending in melee, deflects blow (no damage/Stun/effects). Multiple Parry sources no additional effect.

### Combat Bonus
+1 Combat Skill if: opponent Stunned, OR character has ally within 1". In confined space/doorway: neither side gets ally bonus. Only one Combat Bonus per character.

## Hits
Damage from any source = a Hit. Always resolved same way:
Hit written as +x/+y (Overcome Armor bonus / Overcome Toughness bonus). Default +0/+0.

### Step 1: Overcome Armor
Skip if Armor 0. Roll D6 + Armor bonus.
- Total > Armor: proceed to Step 2
- Total = Armor: Stunned, attack fails
- Total < Armor: attack fails

### Step 2: Overcome Toughness
Roll D6.
- Natural 1: Stunned
- 2-6: add Toughness bonus
  - Total > Toughness: casualty (removed from table)
  - Total ≤ Toughness: Wounded and Stunned

### Stunned
Lost balance/knocked senseless. Must fight as Defender, opponent gets +1 Combat Bonus. Recovers after exchange unless Stunned again. Auto-recovers end of phase.

### Wounded
May continue acting normally. Wounded a second time = removed as casualty. Wounds don't carry to future battles.

## Running Away

### Character Morale
Player forces don't test. Character within 1" of battlefield edge may retreat (remove from table, can't return). May need Flight in the Dark roll.

### Enemy Morale
Tracking Phase: Roll D6 per enemy removed as casualty this round. Natural 1s and 2s = Morale Failures. Per failure, remove one enemy (closest to enemy edge first).
- Leaders don't run unless <4 enemy figures remaining
- Aberrations and Undead fight to death
- Unknown Enemy markers and Unaware enemies unaffected

## Proficiency Tests
Roll 2D6 vs target number. Applicable skill adds +2. Equal or above = success.
Multiple listed skills: any works, only one bonus applies.
Mystics: only +1 from skills (except Alchemy).
Equipment bonus: only one per test. Single-use must declare before rolling.

## Story Points
Begin with some, earn more over campaign. Two uses:
- Narrative: Pay 1 to make something happen (fight specific enemy, old friend shows up, add location). Should not award magic items.
- Mechanical: Avoid attack/spell just struck by, OR reroll any die (keep either result, no third roll).
