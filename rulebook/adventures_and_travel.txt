# Adventures & Travel

## The Adventuring Stage

### Explore a Location
Visit any location on map (Delve, Settlement, Hideout, Camp, Monster Lair, Unexplored Location, etc.). Requires Travel if not in same Map Area. Hidden locations can't be visited.

### Rest Up
Must be in settlement. No battle, no Travel, no XP or rewards. Useful for injured recovery.

### Battle the Enemy Threat
Select Threat to fight. Roll on 3 tables:

Enemy Threat Location Table (D6):
1-3: Current Map Area
4: Near largest settlement
5: Near random settlement
6: Wilderness (new location, Travel required)

Enemy Threat Activity Table (D6):
1: Preparing to attack villagers (+1 AP if Hold Field)
2: Waiting in ambush (all enemies gain Awareness)
3: Returning from raid (+1 to Seize Initiative)
4: Passing through (Unknown Enemy rule)
5: Counting pillage (+1 GM if Hold Field)
6: Hunting your warband (Ranged get Poison)

Enemy Threat End Goal Table (D6):
1: Establish larger presence (fail: D6 4-6 build Camp/upgrade to Hideout)
2: Undermine progress (fail: no Adventure Milestones next turn)
3: Cause chaos (fail: -3 AP)
4: Overall scheme (fail: roll Enemy Plans)
5: Gather information (fail: +1 enemies next fight this Threat)
6: Entrench (fail: D6 > Threat level = increase by 1)

Must reach within current or next turn. Next turn = +1 enemy. Can't reach = treated as loss.

### Ride Patrol
Lower-risk. No Travel roll. Roll D100:
01-15: Traveler Encounter
16-65: Meeting Engagement vs Roadside Enemies (Unknown Enemy)
66-80: Traveler Encounter; if no battle/Contract/Quest, then Meeting Engagement vs Roadside
81-90: Signs of danger - choose to fight random Threat (Meeting Engagement, Unknown Enemy) or roll Enemy Plans
91-100: Wanderer says something odd - add Unexplored Location, may explore now

### Encounter Locations
- Enemy Camps: Staging grounds. Scenario C: Camp Raid. Can become Hidden.
- Enemy Hideouts: Coordinate efforts. Scenario D: Hideout Raid. Can be Hidden.
- Delves: Multiple battles (Depths). Scenario E: Site Battle. Lurking Foes table unless stated otherwise. Depth completion: roll D6, below Depth number = Delve cleared. Delve Depth adjustments table for deeper levels.
- Monster Lairs: 8+ [Scouting] test to breach (once breached, stays). Scenario F: Monster Lair.
- Unexplored Locations: Roll D100 on table when reached.

### Unexplored Locations Table (D100)
01-05: Trouble brewing (Enemy Plans roll)
06-15: Old ruin (Traveler Encounter + add Delve)
16-25: Wilderness (nothing)
26-35: Suspected monster haunt (8+ [Pathwise] = Monster Lair)
36-43: Tunnel complex (add Delve)
44-50: Small community (add hamlet)
51-58: Monster lair
59-63: Enemy camp (random Threat)
64-70: Natural resources (+3 GM)
71-75: Fey woods (optional Site Battle vs Ice-heart Court, +Loot roll if Hold Field, reroll on table)
76-83: Tracks leading to lair (optional Site Battle vs Roadside, +2 AP if Hold Field)
84-89: Haunted site (optional Site Battle vs Whispers, +1 SP + Loot roll if Hold Field)
90-94: Isolated community (hamlet, begin next turn In Town, optional Contract)
95-100: Treasure site (optional Site Battle vs Lurking Foes, 3 extra Loot rolls if Hold Field)

## Travel
Roll D100 on Travel Events Table when moving between Map Areas:
01-30: Uneventful
31-50: Meet someone (Traveler Encounter)
51-65: See something (add Unexplored Location, may go there instead)
66-75: Waylaid (Defensive Battle vs Roadside Enemies, then continue or Camp)
76-85: Miserable weather (9+ [Traveling] or forced to Camp)
86-95: Obstacle (roll on Obstacle Table)
96-100: Lost (Traveler Encounter, Camp, Travel again next turn)

### Obstacle Table (D100)
01-20: River crossing (8+ [Traveling] or lose random Backpack item)
21-35: Marsh (9+ [Scouting] or lose random weapon/shield)
36-50: Broken ground (arrive but can't act further; 8+ [Traveling] to reach settlement)
51-65: Toll post (9+ [Speech], pay 2GM, or re-roll Travel)
66-90: Deep woods (8+ [Pathwise] or lost, Camp, Travel again)
91-100: Unnatural terrain (Meeting Engagement vs Lurking Foes, Unknown Enemy, no Loot/AP, arrive but must Camp)

## Contracts
Generate with two D100 rolls (source + type). Can see details before accepting. Multiple active Contracts OK if from different sources.

### Contract Source Table (D100)
01-14: Individual (1GM + villager Friend)
15-25: Friend/Individual (D3 GM, +1 SP if from Friend)
26-45: Townsfolk (D3+1 GM)
46-70: Town council (D6 GM)
71-80: Secretive group (+3 AP)
81-90: Local noble (D6+2 GM)
91-100: Local clergy (D6 GM + 1 AP)

### Contract Type Table (D100)
01-09: Chase off group | 10-19: Locate item | 20-28: Deliver goods | 29-38: Retrieve item | 39-46: Aid community | 47-56: Rescue individual | 57-65: Escort individual | 66-75: Negotiate deal | 76-83: Gather information | 84-92: Explore area | 93-100: Slay beast

(Each type has detailed rules for location, activity, success condition, and time limit - see main rulebook)

### Secondary Characters
Temporary Followers from contracts: no equipment, may be outfitted. Profile: Agility 1, Speed 4"/+2", CS +0, Toughness 3. Don't count for Upkeep.

## Quests
Multi-encounter connected adventures. Not time sensitive.

### Structure
Series of tasks. After each, roll D6: below completed count = ready for Finale.

### Step 1: Generate Quest Task
Location (D100): 01-20 random existing Map Area, 21-40 new location in existing area, 41-70 new in Wilderness, 71-100 Unexplored Location in Wilderness.

Task types (D100): Various including combat encounters, exploration, rituals, monster hunting, negotiations. Max 2 of same type.

### Step 2: Quest Foes
From unused Threat tables. Each Threat once, then Site Battle Table.

### Step 3: Failures
Failed battle = tally Quest Failure, roll new task. 3 failures = Quest ends.

### Step 4: Quest Finds
After tasks 2, 4, 6 - roll on Quest Find Table (D100) for special rewards.

### Step 5: Quest Finale
Always in Wilderness. Site Battle. Enemy = random current Threat. 6 + Numbers + all leader types. Must defeat Captain+Unique Foe OR center ritual (9+ [Devotion]). 2 attempts allowed.

### Step 6: Quest Rewards
3 Loot rolls, all Heroes +2 XP, Boon of gods (D6: 1-3 +1Luck, 4-6 +1Will), Quest Talent (D100 table with powerful permanent abilities).
